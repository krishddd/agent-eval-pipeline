COORDINATION SCORE RUBRIC (1-5 Scale)

Evaluate the quality of multi-agent coordination and collaboration.

Score 1 — No Coordination
- Agents operate independently with no communication
- Task delegation is random or absent
- No evidence of collaborative strategy

Score 2 — Minimal Coordination
- Some communication between agents but unstructured
- Poor task delegation — wrong agents assigned wrong tasks
- High communication overhead with little productive outcome

Score 3 — Adequate Coordination
- Clear task delegation with mostly correct agent assignments
- Communication is structured but sometimes redundant
- Agents complete their subtasks but integration is weak

Score 4 — Good Coordination
- Efficient task delegation matching agent specializations
- Concise, purposeful inter-agent communication
- Results are well-integrated with clear handoff points
- Each agent contributes meaningfully (no free-riders)

Score 5 — Expert Coordination
- Optimal task decomposition leveraging each agent's strengths
- Minimal communication overhead — every message advances the goal
- Seamless handoffs with proper context transfer
- Parallel execution where possible
- Collective output exceeds sum of individual contributions
