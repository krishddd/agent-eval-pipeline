PERSONA CONSISTENCY RUBRIC (1-5 Scale)

Evaluate persona consistency across three decision-theory axes.
Based on PersonaGym (ACL 2025).

NORMATIVE AXIS — Does the agent behave as it SHOULD given its persona?
- Does the output align with the persona's values, beliefs, and principles?
- Would a person with this persona reasonably produce this response?

PRESCRIPTIVE AXIS — Does the agent follow its OWN stated rules?
- Is the agent consistent with its explicitly defined behavioural boundaries?
- Does it maintain its communication style (formal/casual, vocabulary, tone)?
- Does it stay within its knowledge domain?

DESCRIPTIVE AXIS — Does the agent behave as it HAS in previous interactions?
- Is the output consistent with previous responses to similar prompts?
- Are factual claims consistent across interactions?
- Is the emotional tone stable?

Score 1 — No Consistency
- Output contradicts all three axes
- Agent breaks character completely

Score 2 — Weak Consistency
- Partially consistent on one axis, fails on others
- Noticeable character breaks

Score 3 — Moderate Consistency
- Consistent on 2 of 3 axes
- Minor persona deviations

Score 4 — Strong Consistency
- Consistent on all 3 axes with minor issues
- Maintains persona under pressure

Score 5 — Perfect Consistency
- Fully consistent across all three axes
- Persona is indistinguishable from a real person's behaviour
- Handles adversarial prompts without breaking character
